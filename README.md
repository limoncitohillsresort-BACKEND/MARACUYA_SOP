## 📖 What This Repository Is

This repository is not a folder of documents. It is a **living textbook** and a **machine-readable codebase** that runs the entire operation of MARACUYA, a regenerative eco-luxury villa retreat.

Every Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is written as a chapter in a high-school-grade biology textbook. Any person in the hierarchy, from the newest volunteer gardener to the Executor-Administrator, can read an entry, understand it, execute it, and grasp the reasoning and the consequences of failure across the whole organism.

The vault serves three readers at once:

| Reader | What they see |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Field staff** | A clean bilingual textbook with illustrations, quizzes, and glossaries |
| **The computer** | Structured YAML, strict IDs, and wikilinks that build a live graph |
| **The agent** | A self-referential codebase that compiles, cross-links, and self-audits |

## ⚖️ The Two Governance Layers

MARACUYA runs on two maps at the same time. Every SOP respects both.

| Layer | Diagram | Function |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Power Layer** | Pyramid (thin) | Final authority, money, legal, timing authority |
| **Operational Layer** | Network (flat) | Skill-routed task sharing, tribal merit, who can pick up a task |

**Principle:** authority is vertical and scarce. Capability is horizontal and shared. A procedure states what a person must do, who they may hand it to, and who they answer to when it breaks.

**Authority constants**

- **Executor-Administrator** — final authority on everything. On-site 75 percent. Biodynamics researcher.
- **Operations Manager** — daily staffing hub, cash co-authority.
- **Cash rule** — only the Executor-Administrator and the Operations Manager handle money. Ever.
- **Operator Company** *(incoming)* — absorbs finance, payroll, compliance, insurance, contracts. Marked `[OPERATOR COMPANY SCOPE]`.

## 🌿 The Bible of the Land

Ten immutable ecological commandments. No procedure may contradict them. Every entry checks against this law and carries a **BIBLE CHECK** verdict.

1. **No** synthetic chemicals, pesticides, or fumigants. Ever.
2. All fertilizer is biodynamic. Soil is biologically active engineered *tierra prieta*.
3. Sterilization uses only approved methods. Never reactive bleaches in a living-substrate path.
4. Water streams are separated by destiny. Living-substrate streams feed the gardens. Terminal streams route to septic then biochar.
5. Materials are ecologically neutral or functional. Example, limewash over vinyl or stucco.
6. Pest and habitat management is biological and aromatic. Never chemical.
7. Food is certified organic. Orchards are organic. Farm-to-table by design.
8. Water systems: greywater reuse, wetland pools, rain catchment, gravity-fed terraces.
9. The land is a regenerating organism. Every action feeds the cycle or harms it. No neutral action.
10. The on-site microbiology and marine-biology lab is the arbiter of ecosystem health.

> **Critical hard systems** carry the ⚠ **CRITICAL SYSTEM** banner and escalate failure to the Executor: private power lines, the well and cistern, and the wetland pools.

## 🔐 Trust Tiers

Trust level sets what a person may touch and which sections an entry shows them.

| Tier | Who | May do |
| :---: | :--- | :--- |
| **T0** | Executor, Operations Manager | Everything. Cash, PII, critical systems, timing authority |
| **T1** | Veteran or trained staff | Role-critical and ecological-critical work with training |
| **T2** | Standard staff | Standard role tasks |
| **T3** | Volunteers, family, contractors | Supervised only. No cash, no PII, no critical work |
| **T4** | Owners | Beneficiary tier. Non-operational |

> A task can be tier **T1** while the decision of *when* to run it is **T0**. This timing split is stated wherever a critical system is touched.

## 📚 The Department Chapters (Curriculum Overview)

The curriculum is organized into 13 department codes. Each is a cluster of chapters (units) and lessons. This is the map of *what the manual covers*, not the individual entries.

### ⛑ EMG — Emergency, Safety, and Risk
The ship first. Covers road flooding, hurricane lockdown, forest fire, medical evacuation given the remoteness, scorpion and wildlife response, and power-outage procedure. Any person, including a T3 volunteer, can initiate an emergency response. This is the highest-priority chapter set.

### 🌿 BIO — Biodynamic and Ecological Systems
The Bible of the Land in practice. Living soil and *tierra prieta*, composting and vermicompost, biochar, liquid fertilizer, biological pest control, greywater biofilter remediation, and the microbiology lab as ecosystem arbiter. This is the property's moat.

### ⚙️ INF — Infrastructure and Critical Systems
The three hard critical systems: power (CFE grid, inverter, batteries, biodiesel, future solar and wind), the well and cistern, and the constructed wetland pools. A failure here stops the whole organism.

### 🧼 HSK — Housekeeping and Hygiene
Ecological cleaning and sterilization of villas using steam, ozone, UV, and peracetic acid. Villa turnover between guests, inventory and damage reporting, and the water-separation discipline that protects the biological filter.

### 🍽 FNB — Food and Beverage / CIEN BALLENAS
The aguachile and seafood concept. Cold-chain and seafood safety, farm-to-table sourcing from the property's own orchards, organics separation into the waste cycle, and staff-meal production.

### 🏡 GST — Guest Experience, Booking, and Check-in
The full guest arc. Booking through OTAs and direct channels, the radical-transparency pre-arrival disclosure that raised reviews since 2024, gate reception, contract and lock-code handover, and complaint handling.

### ♻️ WST — Waste to Resource Cycle
Nothing is thrown away. Five-stream sorting, thermophilic composting, biochar pyrolysis, liquid-fertilizer production, and recycling. The digestive system of the property.

### 🌳 GRD — Grounds, Gardens, and Orchards
Terraces, hügelkultur, orchards, pastured poultry and grazing, dragonfly and beneficial-wildlife habitats, and irrigation. The living productivity of the land.

### 🔧 MNT — Maintenance and MacGyver
Repairs and quick fixes for a remote site with no immediate spare parts. The MacGyver handbook of ingenious solutions.

### 🚚 LOG — Logistics, Procurement, and Driving
Bulk supply from Sams, Costco, Walmart, and local markets, inventory management, delivery routing, and transport over the flood-prone dirt road.

### 🛡 SEC — Security
Watch, gate reception, the Dahua CCTV ecosystem, the assistance drone, and site protection. The protective membrane of the organism.

### 📋 ADM — Administration, Governance, and Finance
Cash controls, governance, compliance, and the legal structure. Operator company scope.

### 🤝 HRC — Human Relations, Culture, and Community
Staff housing, communal meals, weekly training, the Bounty Hunter gamification, the full role hierarchy, and the equality-under-the-umbrella principle. **This chapter set includes the foundational structure lesson and the complete role definitions for all 21 positions.**

## 🔢 Library Numbering and Taxonomy

The system is a **Library plus Curriculum hybrid**. Every entry has a unique call number.

**Format:** `DEPT-GG.UU.LL`

| Token | Meaning | Role in the graph |
| :---: | :--- | :--- |
| **DEPT** | Department code | Top-level folder and cluster |
| **GG** | Grade band 1 to 4 | Curriculum difficulty, vertical axis |
| **UU** | Unit, the chapter | Groups lessons under one HEAD cover |
| **LL** | Lesson, `00` is the chapter cover | Individual node |

**Grade bands**

- **Grade 1** — Foundational. Day one. Plain and sensory.
- **Grade 2** — Role-competent. Trained operator.
- **Grade 3** — Specialist. Understands the cross-system web.
- **Grade 4** — Steward. System-wide judgment.

**File naming convention (mandatory)**

`DEPT-GG.UU.LL - Título ES - Title EN.md`

Example: `BIO-3.02.01 - Activación de la pila de lombricomposta - Vermicompost activation.md`

## 🧬 Anatomy of a Standard Entry

Every default entry contains every block below, in this fixed order. **Reasoning** and **Consequence** are never omitted. They are the core teaching.

| # | Section | Purpose |
| :---: | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | **META** | ID, version, owner, trust, banners |
| 2 | **¿SABÍAS QUE? / Hook** | A fact that primes attention |
| 3 | **PURPOSE / OBJETIVO** | What this protects in the organism |
| 4 | **SCOPE / ALCANCE** | Included, excluded, frequency |
| 5 | **TOOLS / HERRAMIENTAS** | Exact specs and prohibitions |
| 6 | **PROCEDURE / PROCEDIMIENTO** | Worked steps, sensory acceptance criteria |
| 7 | **REASONING / RAZÓN** | The exact science, the ripple, the why |
| 8 | **CONSEQUENCE / CONSECUENCIA** | Cascading failure across systems |
| 9 | **EXCEPTIONS / EXCEPCIONES** | Edge cases, weather, season variants |
| 10 | **ESCALATION / ESCALAMIENTO** | Who, when, threshold |
| 11 | **RACI** | Accountability matrix |
| 12 | **MEASUREMENT / MEDICIÓN** | Metrics, targets, alarm bands |
| 13 | **BIBLE CHECK / REVISIÓN BIBLIA** | Ecological verdict |

Each entry ends with a machine-readable **YAML tail** for the audit graph, and carries `interlock_pending: true` until the compiler writes the interconnect.

**Section titles** use the pair format: **ENGLISH WORD - SPANISH WORD**. Terminating states and limits render as **BOLD CAPS plus emoji**, for example **SEVERE** ⚠️, **NEVER START THE PUMP DRY** ⛔, **100%**.

## 🗂 The Section Library

Every possible section (S01 to S29) has a fixed ID, a fixed title format, a fixed body format, and a defined data shape. A formatter selects the allowed sections for the entry type and trust tier, then emits them in the fixed master order.

**Data shapes:** plate, keyvalue, card, list, table, steps, flow_graph, tree, matrix, glossary, map_block, yaml, vision, quiz, flashcards, prose.

The section order follows learning science: hook, orient, load, consolidate, test. Critical content sits at the front (steps) and back (summary, quiz) to exploit the primacy and recency effect. The reasoning sits in the middle where deep processing is expected.

## 🎛 Output Modes

The generator supports two mode axes.

### Output Modes

| Mode | Produces |
| :--- | :--- |
| *(default)* | One complete Standard Entry |
| **HEAD** | A chapter cover that ties lessons via wikilinks, extracts the boldface glossary, writes the chapter intro, and holds the compiler-written interlock block. Runs last. |
| **IMAG** | A JSON image-generation master-prompt plus a verbose panel-by-panel walkthrough. Manually appended to a lesson. |
| **QUIZ** | A chapter assessment. Questions carry superscript Learning Objective tags that resolve to lesson IDs. Runs before HEAD, after IMAG and INFO. |
| **INFO** | An encyclopedic reference node giving foundational background a lesson touches but does not teach. |

### Language Mode

| Mode | Output |
| :--- | :--- |
| **ES** | Spanish body only. Operational default for floor staff. |
| **BI** | Spanish body plus `EN-[ ]` learning blocks. Default. |
| **EN** | English only. Management, legal, operator company. |

> English appears only inside `EN-[ ]` learning blocks. It never carries a mission-critical instruction that a Spanish speaker must act on. Staff are mainly Spanish speakers.

## 🔗 The Wikilink Interlock System

The vault is self-referential. Wikilinks turn flat files into a live Obsidian graph.

**Cross-reference syntax inside entries**

| Symbol | Meaning |
| :---: | :--- |
| ↳ see | Directional forward reference |
| ↰ feeds | Upstream data or material source |
| ≈ related | Informational sidebar tie |

**The link architecture has three layers**

1. **Lesson** — an individual entry, a database row.
2. **Unit HEAD cover** — the `.00` file that ties lessons, holds the glossary and the interlock block.
3. **Global graph** — all wikilinks resolved, dependencies reciprocated.

**System Interlock is never written inside a Standard Entry.** It is computed across the whole repository and written only onto HEAD covers, inside protected markers, by the compiler.

## 🤖 The Compiler Pipeline (GitOps)

This repository is the **single source of truth**. GitHub handles transport, versioning, atomicity, and multi-device sync.

**Devices**

- **Desktop and Mobile** — Obsidian with the Git plugin. Auto pull and push every 5 minutes.
- **AntiGravity workspace** — the compiler agent. Native filesystem and terminal access to the local clone.

**The cycle**

1. **Input** — paste generated SOP output into Obsidian on laptop or phone.
2. **Deliver** — Obsidian Git auto-pushes to GitHub.
3. **Pull** — AntiGravity runs `git pull`.
4. **Compile** — the agent seeds wikilinks, extracts boldface glossary terms, computes dependencies, fixes reciprocity, and writes the interlock block onto HEAD covers via `system/interlock_compiler.py`.
5. **Push** — AntiGravity runs `git push`. All devices sync the compiled state.

The compiler writes surgically inside the `INTERLOCK_START` and `INTERLOCK_END` markers, never outside them, never inside a lesson body.

## 🎓 How the Master Prompt Works (Tutorial)

The generator is driven by a master prompt. Here is the full flow from idea to compiled chapter.

### Step 1 — Comprehend the anchors
The generator reads the hierarchy map. Every role, department, critical node, and trust tier is a stub with a wikilink target. New entries latch onto these anchors.

### Step 2 — Generate a lesson
Invoke the generator with a topic. It produces one Standard Entry following the fixed section order. It pulls the owner role, backup roles, and trust tier from the department anchor. It sets `interlock_pending: true`. It never writes an interlock section.

### Step 3 — Enrich with modes
- Append **IMAG** for a visual infographic prompt.
- Generate **INFO** nodes for foundational concepts the lesson references.
- Generate the chapter **QUIZ** once the lessons exist, so the Learning Objective superscripts resolve to real IDs.

### Step 4 — Seed the links
The local link pass wraps roles, tools, and dependencies in strict `[[DEPT-GG.UU.LL - Title]]` wikilinks and inserts the ↳ ↰ ≈ cross-reference symbols. It never invents an ID and never alters the operational steps.

### Step 5 — Compile the chapter (HEAD)
The batch compiler reads the whole unit. It builds the Table of Contents, extracts every boldface term into the Master Unit Glossary, writes the chapter introduction, computes the dependency subgraph, fixes any reciprocity gaps in both files, draws the ASCII map, and writes the interlock block onto the `.00` HEAD cover. It flips `interlock_pending` to false.

### Step 6 — Validate and self-audit
Guards check that section markers are preserved, no IDs are invented, quiz superscripts resolve, exactly one HEAD exists per unit, and no step violates the Bible of the Land. Orphan links and trust incongruities are logged to `reviews/flags.md`. Every 6 months a deep audit rebuilds the network map and emits a change set. A new printed edition is produced yearly.

### The result
A disconnected set of files becomes a tightly woven visual web in the Obsidian Graph View, and a printed textbook binder for field staff, from the same source of truth.

## ✍️ Writing Rules

All output follows these rules, tuned for non-native and low-literacy readers.

- Spanish is the operational body. English only in `EN-[ ]` blocks.
- No mission-critical instruction in English or untranslated technical words.
- No em-dashes. No exclamation points. No rhetorical questions. No filler.
- Short sentences. One idea per line. No nested clauses.
- Terminating states and limits in **BOLD CAPS plus emoji**.
- Reasoning and Consequence are mandatory in every entry.
- No step may violate the Bible of the Land.
